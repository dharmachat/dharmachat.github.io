Shlokas About Righteousness

Righteousness (Dharma) is the very foundation of Hindu civilization. It is the cosmic order that sustains the universe, the moral law that guides human conduct, and the sacred duty that gives life meaning. These shlokas define what it means to live a life of dharma in every thought, word, and action.

01
धर्मो रक्षति रक्षितः।
Dharmo rakshati rakshitah
✦
Dharma protects those who protect dharma. When you uphold righteousness, the entire universe conspires to protect you.
Mahabharata, Vana Parva
02
धर्मक्षेत्रे कुरुक्षेत्रे समवेता युयुत्सवः।
Dharmakshetre kurukshetre samaveta yuyutsavah
✦
In the field of dharma, at Kurukshetra — the Gita opens by placing dharma at the center of the greatest conflict.
Bhagavad Gita 1.1
03
यतो धर्मस्ततो जयः।
Yato dharmas tato jayah
✦
Where there is dharma, there is victory. Righteousness always prevails in the end, even when it seems defeated.
Mahabharata
04
श्रेयान्स्वधर्मो विगुणः परधर्मात्स्वनुष्ठितात्।
Shreyaan svadharmo vigunah paradharmat svanushthitat
✦
Better is one's own dharma, though imperfect, than another's dharma well performed. Follow your own righteous path.
Bhagavad Gita 3.35
05
सत्यं वद। धर्मं चर।
Satyam vada. Dharmam chara.
✦
Speak the truth. Practice dharma. These two commands from the Upanishads summarize the entire code of righteous living.
Taittiriya Upanishad 1.11
