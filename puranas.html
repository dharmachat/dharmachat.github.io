Privacy Policy

Your trust is sacred to us. Here is how we protect it.

Last updated: March 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to DharmaChat ("we", "our", or "us"). We are committed to protecting your personal information and your right to privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use our website at dharmachat.in and our AI Dharma Guide service.

DharmaChat is built with reverence for Sanatana Dharma and deep respect for our users. We will never sell your data, show you ads, or misuse your information.

2. Information We Collect

When you use DharmaChat, we collect the following information:

  • Your name and email address when you sign in with Google
  • Your Google profile picture (displayed in the app)
  • Your conversation history with the AI Dharma Guide (stored locally in your browser)
  • Your daily question usage count (stored locally in your browser)
  • Basic usage analytics (page visits, feature usage — no personal data)

We do not collect your location, payment information (until you subscribe to Premium), device information beyond what is standard, or any sensitive personal information.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect solely to:

  • Provide you with a personalised DharmaChat experience
  • Display your name and photo in the app
  • Track your daily question usage (10 free questions per day)
  • Improve the quality of our AI responses
  • Send you important updates about the service (only with your consent)

We never use your information for advertising, profiling, or selling to third parties.

4. Google Sign-In

DharmaChat uses Google Sign-In (Firebase Authentication) to verify your identity. When you sign in with Google, we receive your name, email address, and profile picture from Google.

We do not receive your Google password, payment methods, contacts, or any other Google account data. Your Google account remains fully under your control at all times.

You can revoke DharmaChat's access to your Google account at any time by visiting your Google Account settings at myaccount.google.com → Security → Third-party apps.

5. AI Conversations

Your conversations with the DharmaChat AI are processed through Anthropic's Claude API. This means your questions are sent to Anthropic's servers to generate responses. Anthropic's privacy policy applies to this data processing.

Your conversation history is stored only in your browser's local storage — it is not stored on our servers. When you clear your browser data or sign out, your conversation history is deleted.

We do not read, analyse, or store your individual conversations on our systems.

6. Data Storage and Security

DharmaChat uses the following services to store and process data:

  • Google Firebase — for authentication and user management
  • Vercel — for hosting our secure backend API
  • GitHub Pages — for hosting our website
  • Anthropic API — for AI response generation

All data transmission between your browser and our servers is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS. We follow industry best practices for data security.

7. Your Rights

You have the following rights regarding your data:

  • Right to access — you can request a copy of any data we hold about you
  • Right to deletion — you can request deletion of your account and data at any time
  • Right to correction — you can update your information by updating your Google profile
  • Right to withdraw consent — you can sign out and stop using the service at any time

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at sukhilbabu19999@gmail.com.

8. Children's Privacy

DharmaChat is designed for users of all ages who wish to explore Hindu scriptures and dharmic wisdom. However, we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 without parental consent. If you believe a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for legal reasons. We will notify you of any significant changes by posting the new policy on this page with an updated date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

10. Governing Law

This Privacy Policy is governed by the laws of India. Any disputes arising from this policy shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Bengaluru, Karnataka, India.
